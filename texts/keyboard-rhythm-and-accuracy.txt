Typing performance improves when your rhythm is stable. Many learners hit a burst of speed, then pause to fix errors, and repeat the cycle. This creates uneven flow and lower real throughput.

Try a simple rule during practice: keep every line at a controlled tempo that you can maintain without panic corrections. If error rate climbs, reduce speed immediately and restore consistency before pushing faster.

Use short sessions with clear goals. One session can focus on punctuation. Another can focus on number rows or symbol pairs. With this approach, confidence grows and speed becomes reliable.
